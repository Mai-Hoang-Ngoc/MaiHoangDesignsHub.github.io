Redefining Urban Housing : Mixed-Residential Housing Complex

Project Brief: To design 20+ housing units on an existing open-air car park in Cardiff City Centre. Additionally, focusing on adaptive-reuse, sustainability and reducing vehicular dependency.

This project was selected and featured on Dezeen for Cardiff Universities 2023 spotlight projects.

Click here to visit article in Dezeen

Addressing The Housing needs

Background

In todayâ€™s largest cities, the need for affordable and sustainable housing is growing, with demand greatly exceeding supply. The mixed-residential proposal emerges from an existing open-air car park. In the initial stages of the project, site exploration uncovered an abundance of car parks in the city. With a dwindling need for cars in the heavily connected city centre, this presented an opportunity to forge housing for the city's diverse communities.

The project offers 3 housing typologies to cater to different operational and financial needs of growing families, elderly couples and single person households. It targets the population of Cardiff who embrace communal living and seek solace within a close-knit community, defying the prevailing stereotypes surrounding high-density dwellings.
