Mill Lane & Churchill Way Residential Projects: Adaptive Reuse of a Carpark

Project Brief: The adaptation and transformation of an already established 4-story public car park, and open-air car park in the heart of Cardiff Centre. Rotating in site and typology each week, it challenged us to develop innovative proposals that fit the needs of the clients selected. With such well connected locations, I decided that the housing would accommodate a range of users.

Mill Lane

Churchill Way

Background - Mill Lane

The design approach is centered around the preservation of the existing car park infrastructure, serving as the foundation for new living quarters

The scheme has a total of 38 single-level flats, with 1-3 bedrooms each, a shared courtyard, a communal laundry room, a caf√©, and a recreational area. The design responds to the unique site conditions, utilizing offset levels to optimize southern sunlight. The courtyard functions as a transitional zone and shared space for residents to engage, fostering a connection with nature amidst the urban surroundings.

Background - Churchill Way

The aim of this project was to experiment with the tower typology to create housing that would fit into this urban context. Taking inspiration from the One Park Drive in London and the Oasia Hotel in Singapore, the idea was to create an overlapping structure to offer generous balconies and views across the city.

Increasing movement on the ground level was also prioritised, and all previous parking was removed and replaced with open green spaces. Following the existing city grid, two additional access point were introduced to promote permeability and movement.
