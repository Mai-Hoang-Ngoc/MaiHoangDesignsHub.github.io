Saltford Rural Housing Community

Project Brief: To design site-responsive housing for a group of individuals in Saltford with a minimum of 2 housing typologies. The housing will be accessible and accomodate elderly families and growing-families.

Background

Situated within the rural village of Saltford, this project's primary objective is to create sustainable and site-responsive housing in an area prone to flooding and adorned with a variety of native tree species. The preservation of the natural landscape and a focus on the River Avon were central drivers for the project, guiding numerous design choices, including the application of charred timber for the exterior and the meticulous placement and sizing of windows. The project benefits from its proximity to the neighbouring cities of Bristol and Bath, which enabled the exploration of diverse housing typologies.

The scheme encompasses two main housing typologies: a single-story bungalow offering 1-2 bedrooms and a two-story dwelling offering 3-4 bedrooms. Both typologies feature ramped access leading to the heart of the homes, which then branch out to create east-facing living and dining areas, as well as west-facing sleeping quarters. The housing units boast pitched roofs that harmonise with the local architectural style. Notably, the two-story residences incorporate clerestory windows, strategically positioned to maximize passive solar gains and ventilation.
